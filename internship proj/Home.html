Population Growth

Austin is the quickest major metropolitan area in the United States, with a rising population of 34% since 2010. Austin has gained over 579,000 new residents in the last decade, averaging 184 new residents per day.

Population Stats

Between 2010 and 2020, the City of Austin gained nearly 171,465 new residents, according to the US Census Bureau

Austin is located in Travis County and is the 2nd most populated capital city in the U.S., right behind Phoenix

Austin remains one of the top destinations for migrating talent, and first among the top 50 largest metro areas for new residents as a percentage of total population

By 2050 the population of the Austin MSA is projected to more than double in size to more than 4.5 million resident

Austin Real Estate Market

According to National Mortgage News, Austin, Texas, is the fourth hottest housing market in America. The city’s booming tech industry has led to an increase in jobs, which is drawing young professionals to the area.

This in turn makes it very difficult for many new residents to acquire homes. Our job is to help them find a home which matches their set perspectives.

Key Market Stats

Home Values

Home values in Austin have increased by 29.4% over the last year.

Median Sales Price

Median sales price for a home in Austin is $550,000 based on the most recent report from the Austin Board of Realtors.

Market Days

Average days on market for a home in Austin is 15.

Housing Inventory

Months of inventory in the Austin real estate market is just 0.8 months, which means there is about a 5-week supply of homes for sale in the Austin metropolitan area.
